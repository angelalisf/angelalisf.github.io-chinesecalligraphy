Chinese Calligraphy
Chinese calligraphy is the writing of Chinese characters as an art form, combining purely visual art and interpretation of the literary meaning. This type of expression has been widely practiced in China. Calligraphy is considered as one of the four most-sought skills and hobbies in China.

Materials

Here are the fundamental tools you need to write Chinese calligraphy.

Xuan Paper

Shuen paper or rice paper, is a kind of paper that originated in ancient China and is used for writing and painting.

Brush

Modern bamboo brushes are made from various animal hair. The tube of the brush is typically made of bamboo.

Ink

Ink is usually made from lampblack, a sooty residue created by burning pine resin or oil underneath a hood.

Eight Principles of Yong

The Eight Principles of Yong explain how to write the eight fundamental strokes which are found all in the one character, 永 (yǒng). The eight basic strokes are — dian: the dot, heng: the horizontal stroke, shu: the vertical stroke, gou: the hook, ti: the rising strike, wan: the turning stroke, pie: slanting left, and na: slanting right. It was traditionally believed that with frequent practice of these principles as a beginning calligrapher could ensure beauty in one's writing.
